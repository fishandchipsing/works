Addicted Products

A real fictional service of self-sharing things.

What if products didn't need to be owned — what if they could choose their owners instead?

Addicted Products is a thesis project from TU Delft, developed at Haque Design + Research, exploring cybernetics and connected objects through the lens of object agency. The project proposes a scenario where products are shared based on their own needs — not through active human decisions.

The experiment centred on toasters. They were not sold but placed: they would rate potential new hosts, move on if unhappy, and complain if ignored. The service quietly asked what happens when objects have preferences — and whether ownership is really the right model for a world of connected things.

The project was exhibited internationally and sparked conversations about the Internet of Things long before the phrase became a cliché. It won IxDA Best in Show and was covered by Wired, Designboom, and Core77.
