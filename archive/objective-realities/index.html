Objective Realities

Immersive VR stories from the perspective of mundane objects.

What does a smart home look like from inside it — from the object's point of view?

Objective Realities is an immersive VR experience developed with Bruce Sterling. Visitors inhabit a fictional future home not as its residents but as its objects — a Roomba, a fan, a smart plug. From each perspective, they hear the invisible chatter between networked things: their frustrations, routines, and quiet politics.

Built with Unity and Google Cardboard VR, the experience is designed for multiple simultaneous players who switch perspectives to interact as different objects. Developed with automato.farm, the project continues a long exploration into non-human agency and the hidden social life of things.
