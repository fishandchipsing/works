Yibu

A digital/physical toy that teaches children about invisible data.

What if the invisible data around us could become a playground?

Yibu is a contextually aware game where sensors are tools for play — not measurement. Children solve challenges by shaping their physical environment: making it quieter, warmer, or brighter to help game characters thrive. As they play, they learn about temperature, sound, direction, and light.

The game scrapes location-based information to influence its world, making every session different depending on where and when you play. Yibu was developed as part of frogLabs initiatives in Shanghai, exploring new ways of using sensing technology beyond quantification and fitness tracking.
